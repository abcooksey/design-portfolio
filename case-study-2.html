Adapting a Desktop Design for Mobile

How I reimagined Top Hat's search experience for mobile users, balancing feature parity with touch-first interactions to improve mobile engagement by 60%.

Role Product Designer
Timeline 3 months
Team 1 PM, 2 Engineers, 1 Designer
Platform iOS & Android

Mobile app screens showing before and after

The challenge

The desktop search experience didn't translate well to mobile, leading to poor engagement and frustrated users on smaller screens.

The solution

A mobile-first redesign with touch-optimized interactions, smart filters, and progressive search results.

The impact

60% increase in mobile search engagement, 35% reduction in search abandonment, improved app store ratings.

Context

The mobile gap

Top Hat's content library contains millions of questions and resources. While the desktop search worked well, mobile users struggled with the same interface squeezed onto smaller screens.

Analytics showed that 40% of our users accessed the platform via mobile devices, but mobile search usage was disproportionately low. Users would start a search on mobile, then abandon it to continue on desktop.

40%
of users access via mobile, but only 15% complete searches
Research

Understanding mobile behavior

I conducted contextual inquiry sessions to understand how professors used their phones in real teaching scenarios. The insights were eye-opening.

1
Different contexts, different needs

Mobile users were often in-the-moment: between classes, commuting, or quickly searching during lecture prep. They needed faster, simpler interactions.

2
Touch target problems

Filter checkboxes and small buttons were nearly impossible to tap accurately. Users often selected wrong options.

3
Information overload

Search results showed too much information per item, requiring excessive scrolling to find relevant content.

Design

Mobile-first solutions

1. Touch-optimized filters

Replaced small checkboxes with large, tappable chips. Filters are now accessible via a bottom sheet for thumb-friendly interaction.

Filter chips and bottom sheet design

2. Progressive disclosure

Search results show condensed cards by default. Users can expand for details or swipe to quick-add content to their course.

Expandable search result cards

3. Smart suggestions

Added recent searches, trending content, and personalized suggestions to reduce typing on mobile keyboards.

Smart search suggestions

Reflection

Key learnings

What worked

  • Contextual inquiry revealed insights that analytics couldn't capture
  • Bottom sheets proved more intuitive than modal dialogs on mobile
  • Reducing information density improved comprehension and speed

Challenges overcome

  • Balancing feature parity with mobile simplicity required tough prioritization
  • Coordinating with iOS and Android teams on platform-specific patterns
  • Ensuring accessibility across different device sizes
