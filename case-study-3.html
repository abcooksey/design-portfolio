E-Commerce Redesign Strategy

Launching an E-Commerce Platform

How I led the complete redesign of Waterbridge's digital storefront, transforming a dated B2B site into a modern shopping experience that increased conversions by 45%.

Role Lead Product Designer
Timeline 6 months
Team Cross-functional (8 people)
Platform Web (Responsive)

E-commerce homepage and product page designs

The challenge

Waterbridge's existing site was outdated, difficult to navigate, and had poor mobile support, leading to lost sales and customer frustration.

The solution

A ground-up redesign focused on product discovery, streamlined checkout, and a visual identity that reflects brand quality.

The impact

45% increase in conversion rate, 30% reduction in cart abandonment, and significantly improved customer satisfaction scores.

Context

From B2B to B2C

Waterbridge is a premium confectionery brand expanding from wholesale B2B to direct-to-consumer sales. Their existing website was functional for order placement but offered no browsing or discovery experience.

As they pivoted to e-commerce, they needed a platform that could showcase their products beautifully while handling complex inventory, bulk ordering, and both B2B and B2C checkout flows.

Research

Understanding two audiences

The biggest challenge was serving two distinct customer types: B2B buyers placing large recurring orders, and B2C consumers making one-time purchases. I conducted research with both groups.

B2B buyers
  • Need quick reordering of previous purchases
  • Require bulk pricing and quantity discounts
  • Want invoice-based payment options
  • Need delivery scheduling flexibility
B2C consumers
  • Want to browse and discover products
  • Need beautiful product photography
  • Expect fast, simple checkout
  • Value gift options and occasions

"I love Waterbridge products but the website makes it impossible to shop. I end up calling to place orders instead."

Design

Key design decisions

1. Unified yet personalized experience

Created a single platform that adapts based on user type. B2B customers see bulk options and reorder features; B2C customers see lifestyle imagery and gift options.

Personalized homepage variations

2. Product-first visual design

Developed a clean, premium aesthetic that lets product photography shine. Large images, ample white space, and subtle animations create a luxurious feel.

Before

Cluttered, text-heavy listings

After

Visual, browsable product grid

3. Streamlined checkout

Reduced checkout from 5 pages to a single-page experience with smart defaults. Added express checkout for returning customers.

Reflection

What I learned

Key takeaways

  • Serving multiple audiences requires flexibility, not compromise
  • Checkout optimization has outsized impact on conversions
  • Visual design matters enormously for premium brands
  • Mobile-first isn't optional for e-commerce anymore

What I'd do differently

  • Involve development earlier in the design system creation
  • Set up better A/B testing infrastructure from day one
  • Create more detailed documentation for handoff
